Artspace





Artspace is an urban revitalization project that connects land owners with artists in downtown San Francisco. We engage on large scale mural projects in high impact areas, urban blight districts, and anywhere else where large walls could use some loving.

Case study: 1131 Mission St [freespace]

A formerly distressed lot in the shadow of San Francisco's Federal Building, 1131 Mission was completely transformed by two 80x40ft murals, courtesy of local artists Ian Ross and Zio Ziegler, and the help of volunteers.

Parking garage Art Gallery

After watching us paint 1131 Mission St, the adjacent parking lot owner offered his 18 parking bays for further painting (much to our delight).

Within a week, local and international street had converged to create an exterior gallery worthy of exhibition so we capped it off with a party to remember!

do you Want to see more Art in San Francisco?

The Broken Windows Theory states that when streetscapes are left to decline into disrepair, this effects social disorder that spirals downwards in an ever-greater cycle of crime and poverty. We're not so sure how accurate that is, but what we do know is this: investing in local neighborhoods creates community worth living in. Besides, street art makes your coffee taste better.
